Get to know me.

I currently study Economics and International Relations at the Maxwell School at Syracuse University, pursuing concentrations in econometrics, behavioral microeconomics and international political economy. I aim to combine quantitative research methods with qualitative insights to better dissect and enhance the digital human experience. As such, I am deeply interested in human-computer interaction and technological applications within the broader social sciences.
My professional experiences have involved mining and analyzing behavioral data to exploit potential sources of employee retention and workplace productivity, researching cascading behavior in the context of social media networking, developing predictive algorithms, and researching variables on a microeconomic level to explain outliers discovered during quantitative analysis. I've had the pleasure of working and learning alongside extremely innovative people and groups at Ernst & Young, Bloomberg L.P. and Alley.
I play the saxophone, own every game in the Tales series and have watched every episode of the Office at least three times. I'm currently looking for professional and research opportunities concerning data science, analysis and design research.
