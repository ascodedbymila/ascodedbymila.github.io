Esmeralda Murray

I'm a part-time taco pundit and a full-time typography enthusiast who wishes to better explain behavioral phenomena and user experiences through data analysis.

I study Economics and International Relations at the Maxwell School at Syracuse University, pursuing concentrations in econometrics, behavioral economics and international political economy.

My research concerns time-inconsistent instances of utility discounting in the context of help-seeking rates and the effect of accessible technology on intertemporal choices. I work through the NEXIS Lab at the iSchool at Syracuse.

I've worked and learned alongside some amazing people at Ernst & Young, Bloomberg and Alley and will be joining BlackRock's Portfolio Analytics Group as a Summer Analyst next year. Check out my resume.
